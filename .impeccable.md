## Design Context

### Users
Families researching where to relocate — browsing at home, often evenings, making one of the biggest decisions of their lives. Secondary: real estate agents showing neighborhoods to clients during consultations.

### Brand Personality
Reassuring, grounded, clear. A calm expert who makes complex data feel simple and trustworthy — not a military ops center, not a gimmicky map app.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Trustworthy and calming — Airbnb/Wise territory
- **Theme**: Both light and dark, user-toggled
- **Anti-references**: Military HUD, monospace-everything, cyan/amber neon, sci-fi brackets, noise overlays, sharp edges
- **References**: Airbnb (warmth, whitespace), Wise (clarity, confidence), Zillow/Redfin (data presentation for home decisions)

### Design Principles
1. **Data should comfort, not overwhelm** — present neighborhood insights as clear, digestible cards, not dense readouts
2. **The map is the hero** — everything else supports the map, not competes with it
3. **Warmth over precision** — rounded corners, soft shadows, friendly type. Families, not analysts
4. **Both modes are first-class** — dark mode isn't an afterthought, light mode isn't the only option
5. **Agent-presentable** — clean enough that a RE agent can screen-share with a client and look professional
